What I Do
I specialize in composing highly melodic and narrative-driven music in the style of classic JRPGs such as Final Fantasy, the Chrono series, Tales series and those like them. What made these games special wasn’t just that their music was good; their music was the backbone of their worlds and stories.

While a lot of music takes a passive role in storytelling and worldbuilding, simply blending into the background, the style of these classic series as well as my own take an active role in building the world and telling the stories therein. Good music is easy to come by, but music that makes its listeners want to go to YouTube years later and relive the game and their memories with it isn't.

With this in mind, my bottom line is to make music that is a selling point and integral part of the identity of the games I work on, not just pleasant but quickly forgettable background noise. I strive to make music that will help games become cherished and important emotional experiences for their players.

They say true joy is composed of a tear and a smile, and anything less won't do.
FAQ

A: Very generally speaking, "passive" music is the kind you hear in most movies: appropriate, but a bit forgettable because it's creating a texture in that immediate moment more than trying to tell its own story. In a sense, "passive" music "agrees" with the scene but contributes little of its own to the narrative.

"Active" music, on the other hand, not only "agrees" with the scene or surroundings, but gives its own perspective for a more emotionally and imaginatively complete package.

My name’s Geoff Butterworth, also known as Vermanubis, and it’s a pleasure to meet you.

Game music has been my life since I was 5 years old when I would play all the way through Final Fantasy: Mystic Quest just to jam to the final dungeon theme. As I got older, I started to realize that game music got into my heart and made me feel in ways no other kind of music came close to.

Since about that age it's been my life's work to understand and perfect what makes game music so special and to get others to feel what my most beloved games made me feel in my own work. If you can see that fact reflected in my work and want music that will stay with your players forever, I can't overstate how happy I'd be to help you make a game that'll mean something to people.
